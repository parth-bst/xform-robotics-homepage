TRANSFORMERS. LIVING IN A WORLD FILLED WITH EXTENDABLE AND CONNECTED ROBOTS.

Bipedal

Two legs with mountable wheels, LCD display

  • Basic house assistant
  • Like a robotic pet
  • Talk to you and monitor health
  • Call someone in emergencies
  • Wireless battery charger on bum
  • Sits on charging mat like a pet

Tripedal

Bi-pedal with additional arm with gripper, antenna and stereo camera

  • Controls other robots
  • Huge hip with display, CPU/GPU, battery
  • Wireless battery charger on bum
  • Can plug charger or USB cable
  • Mounts on semi-autonomous platform

Quad Pedal

Four legs, vision, wheels for better grip, antenna, LCD, display/CPU

  • Dog shaped or 2 legs and 2 arms
  • More advanced configuration
  • Enhanced stability and mobility
  • Versatile for various terrains

Hex Pedal

Six legs, LCD display

  • Arms can be modded with clippers or wheels
  • Based on usecase/need
  • Maximum stability and flexibility
  • Ideal for complex environments

ROBOVERSE - Robots can combine and work together in the universe.

Semi-autonomous Platform

Wheeled body, LCD screen, platform. Bipedal and tripedal can self-mount on the platform, grip with legs and use camera & control or clipper gripper for fleet-based search and grab operations.

Big Drone Module

One pedal with camera vision, bi/tri/quad/hex pedals – lifted/mounts the drone. Robot and drone combine capabilities (combined 360°+depth vision, navigation and other sensors) to navigate difficult, unknown or precision demanding environments.

  • When exploring unknown caves, drone and Hex (4 leg + 2 clipper) config provides robust flying and walking capabilities
  • Bi or tri-pedal can control drone module and provide user or robot planned waypoints and routes
  • Bi-pedal and drone together captures family photographs or cinematographic videos

Real-World Examples & Research

Whleaper: 10-DOF Flexible Bipedal Wheeled Robot

Research from Tsinghua University - This innovative robot demonstrates exactly what we're advocating for: robots that don't need to look like humans but are designed for optimal functionality.

Key Features

  • 10 degrees of freedom (5 DOFs per leg)
  • 3 DOFs at each hip joint (like humans)
  • Combines wheeled efficiency with legged adaptability
  • Enhanced stability and flexibility

Capabilities

  • Walking, jumping, and obstacle avoidance sliding
  • Seamless transitions between locomotion modes
  • Superior terrain adaptability
  • Complex motion execution
Why This Matters:

Whleaper proves that increasing degrees of freedom at the hip joint (similar to human anatomy) significantly enhances robot stability and flexibility. This research validates our philosophy: robots should be designed for their intended function, not to mimic human appearance.

Reference: Zhu, Y., et al. "Whleaper: A 10-DOF Flexible Bipedal Wheeled Robot." arXiv preprint arXiv:2504.21767v1 (2025).
